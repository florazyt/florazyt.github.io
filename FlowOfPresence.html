The Flow of Presence

Interactive Installation
Data Visualization

Duration:
Sept. 2024 - Apr. 2024 

A responsive interface that visualizes the temporal presence of people within a space of passing. It aims to create and preserve memories, add vibrancy to the emptiness, and encourage participation. The project reacts to directionality, speed, behaviors, and senses, preserving these missed occurrences.

Team

Claire Li
Yanting Zhou

My Role

Spatial Researcher
Interaction Designer
Prototyper

Tool

Unity
Figma

Context

ArtCenter is celebrating the 20th anniversary of the College's South Campus. The new building welcome students, faculty, and alumni to show their work.

In the hallway of the building, our whole team is invited to design an interactive hallway installation together that leverages the following mixed reality technologies: augmented reality, computer visions, and Unity, a spatial interactive design software. 

Solution

  • Clock interface
    Visualizes hallway traffic from 8am to 8pm daily.

  • Motion capture
    Warm and cold colors indicate walking direction.

  • Interactive data
    Movements recorded and influence existing data.

  • Daily color variation
    Unique colors for each day of the week.

Technology-Centered Research - Camera Deployment Testing

Takeaways

  • Communication is key. Working with Claire was a valuable lesson in communication. She demonstrated the importance of knowing when and how to seek help from professionals when facing design or technical challenges.

  • A clear division of labor is crucial for success. Recognizing that no one is perfect, we leveraged each team member's strengths and weaknesses, producing better work collectively than any individual could alone.

  • A good framework doubles the effect and halves the effort.  By thinking ahead and establishing a structured workflow, we ensured clarity in our direction and achieved more controllable results.
