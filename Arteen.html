Personal UX Project

Duration:

Arteen is an app designed for teenagers to learn arts and the history. It has multiple functions to fit different needs revolves around art study.

User could easily access the grand database of artworks, read the stories behind authors, or learn how to paint and craft through video tutorial. They can also have their great works rated! There are special features provide novel ways of observing art: a glimps of art history through animation and exploration of local art museum with augmented reality.

Specification

User research, User flow, Information architecture, Wireframe, Interactive prototype, User Testing

Tool

Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, After Effect, Figma.

Challenge

Design an approachable app for children aged 10 to 18 and teenagers who is interested in art to learn about art knowledge in a fun and interactive way. 

Design Process

Outcome

Not only do children want to know how to make art, but they are also curious about discovering in art history. This holistic approach to art education fosters a deeper appreciation and a more profound understanding, encouraging children to see art as a living dialogue between the past and the present.

Feature 1

Art Discovery

Children can explore artworks in various ways.

Feature 2

History Exploration

Masterpiece-inspired interactive illustration​ to travel back in time.

Feature 3

Artwork Story

Reconmmend artworks base on children's taste​.
Interesting stories behind each masterpiece in a conversational tone.

Feature 4

Augmented Museums

Children can explore artworks in museums​ with AR feature for a better understanding of the exhibition.

Design System

Define Problem

1. Non-nutritive information in Internet occupy the time of teens, which is negative to their growth of ego.

2. Exhibition introduction is less and not vivid, though visiting museums is becoming popular, teens cannot understand it well.

3. Limited teaching methods of Chinese art courses block teens from contacting real art even if they are interested.

User Research

In order to study the current situation of art education of teenagers, I conducted a survey on 137 art and non-art students aged from 12 to 22 in Nanjing. Results are as follows:

Art students think their course of learning has limitation and expect more art tutorial; Non-art students complain about their experience in art gallery and exhibition and expect more art knowledge; Both of the students show interest in art, they are frequent phone user and get to know art from the Internet.
